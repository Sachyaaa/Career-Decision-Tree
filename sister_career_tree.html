🔄 If She Drops Out or Falls Behind in Distance MBA

  1. If falling behind: Switch from regular to lateral admission — IGNOU allows you to re-enroll in later semesters if you miss one. Flexibility is built in.
  2. If job is too demanding: Reduce course load — most distance programs allow you to take 1 subject per semester instead of 3-4. It takes longer (3–4 years) but she completes it.
  3. If completely dropping out: Don't. A partial distance MBA has near-zero value. Either complete it slowly or switch to a shorter PG Certificate (PGDM, Diploma) which takes 1 year instead of 2.
  4. Alternative: If she grows at the job rapidly and doesn't need the degree — pause enrollment (most allow this) and resume when career levels off. Many do this.

If distance MBA isn't working and she wants campus MBA — she now has 1–2 years experience, which qualifies her for "working professional" MBA quota at many tier-2 colleges. Experience-based admission, easier than fresher quota.
